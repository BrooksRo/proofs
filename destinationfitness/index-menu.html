The REAL Truth About Getting Into Shape

There are many lies, myths and misconceptions that you have been subjected to in regards to getting into shape. Society and the media portray the "most effective" fat loss and muscle building solutions in advertisements ranging from magazines to the largest soundboard on earth, the internet. But the fact of the matter is, the people behind these so called "solutions" have an agenda, and that agenda DOES NOT have your best interest at heart. Allow me to clear up some of the most popular misconceptions that you have been exposed to by society and the media:

  • Cardio is the optimal way to burn fat
  • Women should not weight train because they will get "bulky"
  • The only way to get and stay lean is to stay on a very low calorie diet
  • You can burn fat covering a specific muscle by exercising that muscle group
  • Women shouldn't try to attain muscle, they should only concern themselves with burning fat
  • Diets are all restrictive and bland

Through my own trials and tribulations, I too have believed many of the popular myths that are floating around society today. If I didn't get into the trenches MYSELF to see how fitness really works, I might have never been able to distinguish the real truth from all the lies. It's not your fault that the media sends out useless and unhealthy programming regarding fitness. If I did not spend the last decade of my life devoting my time to exercise and nutrition, I might still be in the dark as well. Let's take a closer look at these fitness myths:

  • Cardio is the optimal way to burn fat. FALSE. People who spend hours upon hours in aerobic style classes, on the treadmill or elliptical, ARE NOT ACCOMPLISHING ANYTHING, but allowing your metabolism to stay at low levels and burn muscle. Not only are you wasting your precious time by not utilizing your fat stores during this type of exercise, but you are actually doing your body a great disservice by burning lean muscle mass. And lean muscle mass happens to BURN CALORIES 24 HOURS A DAY, 7 DAYS A WEEK, EVEN WHILE YOU ARE SLEEPING!  The key to burning fat is to increase your metabolism through resistance training and specialized high intensity interval exercises. The metabolism increase one attains from such exercise techniques is what will allow your body to BURN FAT 24 HOURS A DAY, 7 DAYS A WEEK, EVEN WHILE YOU ARE SLEEPING! 
  •  Women should not weight train because they will get "bulky". FALSE. Weight training does NOT cause women to become bulky. Weight training MUST be executed in a certain way for any muscle to be accumulated, let alone for that muscle to appear bulky. Just think about all of the men slaving away in the gym, TRYING to put on significant muscle mass, and DON'T. This is possibly the biggest misconception out of them all. Women can train with lighter weights, strict form, high reps, low rest periods and build beautiful, aesthetic bodies. There are also physiological reasons as to why women will not take on a muscle bound physique, such as lower testosterone and higher estrogen levels. Women just do NOT have the hormones to build a significant amount of muscle and when looking to attain a lean and toned physique, the workout routine will NOT be aimed at building bulk! In fact, the female bodybuilder image that comes to mind when a woman thinks about lifting weights is EXACTLY the OPPOSITE of what you will look like after taking part in an effective weight training regimen. Women can and do attain aesthetic, sculpted bodies through resistance training!  
  • The only way to get and stay lean is to stay on a very low calorie diet. FALSE. When on an effective nutrition plan, you will actually be eating MORE than you normally do, leaving you feeling more satiated as well. Low calorie diets are harmful to your health and overall wellbeing.
  • You can burn fat covering a specific muscle by exercising that muscle group. FALSE. Spot reduction is physiologically, IMPOSSIBLE. Working out an area of your body (i.e. Abdominals) will strengthen the muscles UNDERNEATH whatever it is that is covering it (i.e. Fat, subcutaneous water). Therefore, if you have high levels of body fat, you will not see your abdominals any better than before you decided to exercise them.
  • Women shouldn't try to attain muscle, they should only concern themselves with burning fat. FALSE. Lean muscle will accentuate the natural, feminine curves of the female body and keep your metabolism elevated 24 hours a day.
  • Diets are all restrictive and bland. FALSE. Instead of using the word "diet" which has many negative connotations, I like to use the phrase "nutrition plan" instead. Nutrition plans that are meant to last a lifetime are by NO means bland or tasteless. There is much room for indulging in foods that you enjoy and flavoring the healthy foods that you consume on a consistent basis to your liking. There is a wide variety of foods out there that are both healthy and delicious when prepared correctly. Being healthy is all about living a fitness lifestyle. Eating foods you do not like will get you about as far as your will power will take you until you eventually binge. This is what we look to avoid as fitness professionals. In fact, when on a strategically structured nutrition plan, you can expect to no longer crave foods that drain you of energy and store body fat. The body begins to crave foods that make your body feel energized and no longer does it crave foods that will send your energy levels on a rollercoaster ride.

Throughout my life, I have been at both ends of the body composition spectrum. During my teenage years, I went from being 120 lbs. of just skin and bone to a muscular, aesthetic 190 lbs. At a later date however, I suffered a series of very traumatic events which left me immobile for a long period of time.
